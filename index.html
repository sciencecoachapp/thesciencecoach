Example Session

What a session looks like

Every session is tailored — but here's a typical 60-minute GCSE Biology session to give you a clear picture of what to expect.

0–5 mins
Check-in & Review
We start with a quick check on how the previous week went at school — any new topics covered, any homework that raised questions, anything that didn't make sense in class. This keeps sessions connected to what's actually happening in school right now.
5–20 mins
Concept Explanation
The target topic is introduced or revisited using a shared digital whiteboard. Diagrams are drawn live — cell structures, biological processes, graphs — so the student sees the thinking, not just the finished result. Common misconceptions are tackled directly rather than avoided.
20–40 mins
Exam Question Practice
Past paper questions are worked through together on screen. We annotate the question first — identifying command words, marking allocations, what the examiner is actually asking for. The student attempts the answer, then we compare against the mark scheme line by line. This is where most marks are found.
40–55 mins
Technique Refinement
We identify the specific patterns in the student's answers — where they're losing marks, what language they're using that the examiner doesn't credit, what they know but aren't writing down. This is the most valuable part of any session and the thing most classroom teachers don't have time to do.
55–60 mins
Summary & Homework
A short recap of what was covered, one or two focused practice questions to try before the next session, and a note of what we'll work on next time. Parents receive a brief written summary after every session so they're always in the picture.
